Question 1: What is the primary purpose of a digital signature?

Your Answer:
✓ To provide authentication and non-repudiation
Explanation: Digital signatures provide both authentication (verifying the sender) and non-repudiation (preventing the sender from denying they sent the message).

Question 2: Which algorithm is commonly used for RSA key generation?

Your Answer:
✗ SHA-256 hashing
Correct Answer:
✓ Prime number generation and modular arithmetic
Explanation: RSA relies on the mathematical difficulty of factoring large prime numbers. SHA-256 is a hashing algorithm, not used for RSA key generation.

Question 3: What is the key size typically used for RSA encryption today?

Your Answer:
✓ 2048 bits or higher
Explanation: Modern RSA implementations use 2048-bit keys as the minimum standard, with 4096-bit keys recommended for high-security applications.
